Order books & market making in 2 minutes

📖 The book

Bids (buyers) stack below, asks (sellers) above. The gap is the spread; the size at each price is depth. Deep book = hard to move; thin book = swings easily.

🤝 Maker vs taker

A limit order rests and makes liquidity. A market order crosses the spread and takes it — getting immediacy but paying the spread (and slippage on big size).

⚙️ Matching

Price–time priority: best price first, then first-come-first-served. Trades print at the resting (maker) price. Watch a market order walk the book in the Live book scene.

💹 Market making

Quote both sides, earn the spread — but manage inventory risk (fills push you off flat) and adverse selection (informed traders pick off stale quotes). That's the whole job.

🧠 Avellaneda–Stoikov

Skew quotes around an inventory-adjusted reservation price and widen the spread with volatility. Try the Market maker scene and move the γ / k / σ sliders.

🔎 The honest caveat

"The order book predicts the next move" is mostly false: order-flow imbalance is contemporaneous (R²≈0.33), not predictive (≈0.00). It describes the move as it happens; it doesn't forecast it.
